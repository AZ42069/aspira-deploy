Questions

What is a Merchant Cash Advance (MCA)?

An MCA is an advance on a business’s future receivables. The business receives capital upfront and repays daily via ACH. It’s not a loan — it’s a purchase of future receivables, secured by a UCC lien. Aspira underwrites, funds, and collects these advances, generating returns that are distributed to investors monthly.

How are my returns generated?

Aspira funds MCAs at factor rates averaging 1.65x. A $10,000 advance collects back ~$16,500 over an average term of 114 days via daily ACH. After accounting for write-offs (3.56% historical rate), the margin is distributed to investors as preferred monthly returns of 12–18% annually, depending on tier.

What happens if a merchant defaults?

Every deal is secured by a UCC lien on the merchant’s receivables. Defaults are managed through an internal restraints team, Triton Recovery Group (third-party collections), and legal enforcement. Additionally, a 12% first-loss cash reserve provides a buffer before investor capital is affected.

Who qualifies to invest?

This offering is available to accredited investors only: $200K+ annual income ($300K joint), or $1M+ net worth excluding primary residence. Verification takes under 5 minutes through VerifyInvestor.com.

What is the minimum investment?

$50,000. Investment tiers are $50K (12% annual), $100K (14%), $250K (16%), and $500K+ (18%). All tiers receive monthly preferred distributions.

How is my investment structured?

Your capital is held in a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) — a legally separate entity from Aspira’s operating company. This ring-fences your investment. Securities are placed through Zastre & Co LLC, a Series 82 FINRA licensed placement agent.
